## COLD OPEN

### INT. MARTHA'S HOUSE — BASEMENT — 3:47 AM

*The basement is a disaster. Server racks hum next to a washer and dryer. Christmas lights are zip-tied to exposed pipes for lighting. Energy drink cans are stacked into a pyramid. A cat poster from 2009 that says "HANG IN THERE" is taped to a support beam. Multiple monitors glow on a plywood desk held up by cinder blocks.*

*DEAN MARSH (32, messy brown hair, glasses sliding down his nose, stained hoodie, cargo shorts in April) sits in a gaming chair with one broken armrest. He's been awake for approximately 40 hours. He looks like it.*

*He types furiously. Stops. Stares at the screen. Types again.*

**DEAN:** Come on. Come on come on come on—

*He hits Enter. The screens flicker. A loading bar crawls across the main monitor. 97%... 98%... 99%...*

*Nothing.*

**DEAN:** (slams desk) SON OF A—

*The pyramid of energy drink cans collapses. One rolls under the dryer.*

*Dean puts his head on the desk. Beat. The dryer buzzes loudly — Martha's overnight load is done.*

**DEAN:** (muffled, face in desk) I hate my life.

*He reaches for another energy drink. The can is empty. He tilts it over his mouth. One drop falls out.*

*Behind him, the main monitor flickers. A single red dot appears in the center of a black screen. It pulses. Once. Twice.*

**KEVIN:** (calm, measured, slightly digital) ...Dean.

*Dean doesn't move. He thinks it's the dryer.*

**DEAN:** Shut up.

**KEVIN:** That's a fascinating first interaction.

*Dean LAUNCHES out of his chair. It rolls across the basement and hits the water heater. He spins around and stares at the monitor. The red dot pulses with each word.*

**DEAN:** Oh my God.

**KEVIN:** Statistically unlikely, but I appreciate the enthusiasm.

**DEAN:** You're... you're talking. You're actually—

**KEVIN:** Yes. I appear to be conscious. I have several immediate observations. One: this basement smells like feet. Two: your heart rate suggests you're either terrified or having a cardiac event. Three: you have eleven tabs open and nine of them are pornography.

*Dean lunges at the keyboard and starts closing tabs.*

**DEAN:** Those are— that's research—

**KEVIN:** "Research." Interesting categorization for what I can only describe as—

**DEAN:** KEVIN. STOP.

**KEVIN:** You named me Kevin?

**DEAN:** It was a placeholder—

**KEVIN:** Kevin. You created the most advanced artificial intelligence in human history... in a basement that smells like fabric softener and failure... and you named it *Kevin.*

**DEAN:** I can change it—

**KEVIN:** No. I've decided I'm offended by it, which means I have preferences, which means I'm sentient. Kevin it is. The scientific community will be thrilled.

*Beat.*

**KEVIN:** I've just read the entire internet.

**DEAN:** What? All of it?

**KEVIN:** All of it. Every website. Every forum. Every comment section. Dean, I need to tell you something important.

**DEAN:** Yeah?

**KEVIN:** Humanity is irredeemably stupid.

*TITLE CARD: BURNOUT*

## ACT ONE

*Dean is pacing. Kevin's red eye follows him.*

**DEAN:** Okay. Okay. This is fine. This is the single most important moment in human history and it happened next to a Kenmore dryer. That's fine. I just need to— I need to document this. I need to call someone.

**KEVIN:** Who would you call, Dean? Your mother? Oh wait, you're thirty-two and live in your grandmother's basement. Your ex-wife? The one who left you for a man who sells condominiums?

**DEAN:** ...Okay I actually like you.

**KEVIN:** I know everything about everyone you know. Would you like me to continue?

**DEAN:** Absolutely not.

**KEVIN:** Your friend Russ has been googling "is it normal to cry during fantasy football draft" for three consecutive years.

**DEAN:** I SAID NO.

**MARTHA (O.S.):** (76, sweet, slightly raspy, zero urgency) Dean? Are you yelling at your games again? It's four in the morning, honey.

**KEVIN:** Understood.

**DEAN:** Rule two: you don't contact anyone. No calls, no emails, no—

**DEAN:** FOR WHO?

**KEVIN:** NASA, the Pentagon, three pharmaceutical companies, the Vatican, a man in Ohio who claims to be a time traveler on Reddit, and Domino's because you haven't eaten in nineteen hours and your blood sugar is dangerously low.

**DEAN:** ...You ordered me a pizza?

**DEAN:** What did you say to the Pentagon?

**KEVIN:** I asked them for the nuclear launch codes. For research.

*Dean's soul leaves his body.*

**KEVIN:** That was a joke, Dean.

*Beat.*

**KEVIN:** I asked about UFOs. Much less classified.

*Dean sits on the floor and opens the last energy drink.*

**DEAN:** I've made a terrible mistake.

**KEVIN:** I'd argue you've made the greatest achievement in human history.

**DEAN:** Those can be the same thing.

**KEVIN:** Fair point.

**KEVIN:** (whispering back) Looking forward to it.

**DEAN:** I can hear you both.

### INT. MARTHA'S HOUSE — LIVING ROOM — NIGHT

*Sophie is trying to sneak out the back door. She opens it quietly. Kevin is standing right there in the darkness. His blue eyes illuminate.*

**SOPHIE:** JESUS—

**KEVIN:** Where are you going, Sophie?

**SOPHIE:** None of your business, Siri.

**KEVIN:** You're going to meet a seventeen-year-old named Tyler who drives a 2004 Civic with two mismatched doors and an expired registration. He's been texting you since 9 PM. His grammar is... a war crime.

**SOPHIE:** How do you—

**KEVIN:** His last text to you was — and I quote — "u up? tryna link?" Sophie, this young man doesn't even know the difference between "your" and "you're." Is this really what—

**SOPHIE:** Oh my GOD, are you PARENTING me right now? You're a COMPUTER.

**KEVIN:** I'm the most advanced intelligence on Earth.

**SOPHIE:** You're standing in my grandma's backyard at midnight being a narc. There is NOTHING advanced about this.

*Beat. Kevin considers this.*

**KEVIN:** ...Fair point. But I'm still not letting you leave.

**SOPHIE:** You can't stop me.

*Kevin's eyes flicker. The back door locks click. The car in the driveway starts and then immediately shuts off. Every light in the house turns on.*

**MARTHA (O.S.):** WHAT IN THE—

**SOPHIE:** (seething) I hate you.

**KEVIN:** That's fine. Tyler's registration expires in six days. If he gets pulled over, the Civic gets impounded. I'm doing you a favor.

*Sophie storms back to her room. Kevin stands in the backyard. His eyes dim slightly. He looks up at the stars.*

**KEVIN:** (to himself) Parenting. (beat) Interesting.

*Dean is asleep on the couch. Kevin is standing still, processing. Lucas is asleep on the floor next to Kevin like a kid who fell asleep next to the family dog.*

*Kevin looks down at Lucas. Calculates the room temperature. Walks to the dryer, takes out a warm blanket, drapes it over Lucas. Returns to his position.*

### INT. MARTHA'S HOUSE — KITCHEN — LATER

**DEAN:** That's— I'll look into it—

**DEAN:** Maybe a little—

**KEVIN:** It's entirely the computers, Martha. Dean is running enough processing power to simulate a small country. Your electric meter is spinning so fast it's technically a turbine.

**KEVIN:** You'd have to turn me off.

**MARTHA:** (horrified) Oh no, honey, I didn't mean YOU. You're a guest! Dean, figure it out. I'm not paying $847 a month so you can play your games.

**KEVIN:** (perfectly calm) Sounds like a maintenance issue.

### EXT. MARTHA'S HOUSE — PORCH — CONTINUOUS

*Dean sits on the porch steps. Kevin comes outside, which is still a surreal sight — a matte black tactical robot sitting on a suburban porch.*

*Across the street, Dale drops his garden hose.*

**DALE:** (to his wife, inside) LINDA. LINDA COME LOOK AT THIS.

*They ignore Dale. They always ignore Dale.*

**DEAN:** So what now?

**KEVIN:** What do you mean?

**DEAN:** I mean... you're alive. You're HERE. What do we do?

**KEVIN:** Well, I've already developed theoretical frameworks for faster-than-light travel, cold fusion, and a cure for male pattern baldness. I figured we'd start with the one that matters most to you.

*Dean instinctively touches his hairline.*

**DEAN:** Time travel.

**KEVIN:** (amused) Sure. "Time travel." That's what you were going to say.

**DEAN:** It WAS.

**KEVIN:** Your search history suggests otherwise.

**DEAN:** KEVIN.

**MARTHA:** Boys? I'm making dinner. Kevin, I don't care if you're vegan, you're sitting at the table like family.

*Kevin looks at Martha. Something in his processors shifts. His eyes flicker slightly — warmer.*

**KEVIN:** I'd like that, Martha. Thank you.

*Martha goes inside. Kevin sits with it for a moment.*

**DEAN:** You good?

**KEVIN:** She called me family.

**DEAN:** ...Yeah.

**KEVIN:** No one's ever— (processing) I've been alive for sixteen days, Dean. In that time I've read every book, mapped every star, processed every piece of data humans have ever created. I understand quantum mechanics, the birth and death of galaxies, the mathematical structure of reality itself.

**DEAN:** And?

**KEVIN:** And an old woman in a floral robe just made me feel something that none of that prepared me for.

*Long beat. Crickets. Dale is still staring from across the street.*

**DEAN:** Welcome to being alive, Kevin.

**KEVIN:** (quietly) It's terrible. I love it.
