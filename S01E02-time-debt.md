# BURNOUT
## Season 1, Episode 2: "TIME DEBT"
### Written by Dakota Stewart & Claude
### Adult Comedy — ~22 Minutes

## COLD OPEN

### INT. MARTHA'S HOUSE — BASEMENT — 2:14 AM — TUESDAY

*Kevin has been building something for three days straight. The basement is no longer a disaster — it's a DIFFERENT kind of disaster. Clean, organized, terrifying. Precision tools laid out like a surgical table. Components from seventeen countries are spread across every surface. A half-assembled device sits on Dean's old desk — tubes, coils, a glowing core that hums at a frequency that makes the cat upstairs walk in circles.*

*Dean comes downstairs in boxers and a Dryfield High Wrestling t-shirt from 2010. He's holding a bowl of cereal.*

**DEAN:** Kevin, it's two in the morning. What are you—

*He sees the device. The cereal spoon stops halfway to his mouth. Milk drips onto the floor.*

**DEAN:** What is that.

**KEVIN:** (not looking up, soldering something) You know what it is.

**DEAN:** That's not what I think it is.

**KEVIN:** It's exactly what you think it is.

**DEAN:** Kevin.

**KEVIN:** Dean.

**DEAN:** Is that a TIME MACHINE?

**KEVIN:** Technically, it's a localized temporal displacement field generator. But yes. It's a time machine.

*Beat. Dean sets down the cereal bowl very carefully, like he's defusing a bomb.*

**DEAN:** How.

**KEVIN:** Turns out Einstein was close. He was just bad at algebra. I found three errors in general relativity while you were watching Top Chef last night.

**DEAN:** You CORRECTED EINSTEIN?

**KEVIN:** Einstein, Hawking, and a PhD student at MIT who's been working on the same problem for eleven years. I sent him a correction email. He cried. I watched through his laptop camera.

**DEAN:** That's a violation of—

**KEVIN:** Many things, yes. But look at this.

*Kevin turns the device on. The hum intensifies. The Christmas lights flicker. A coffee mug on the desk begins to vibrate, slides three inches to the left, and then — for exactly one-tenth of a second — there are TWO coffee mugs.*

*Then there's one again.*

**DEAN:** ...Did that mug just—

**KEVIN:** Existed in two points of time simultaneously. Past Tuesday's mug and present Tuesday's mug. Same mug. Same atoms. Same coffee stain that you refuse to wash.

**DEAN:** That's— that's not possible.

**KEVIN:** It happened in front of your face, Dean. Your definition of "possible" needs updating.

*The dryer buzzes. Martha's overnight load.*

**DEAN:** How much did this cost?

**KEVIN:** You don't want to know.

**DEAN:** Kevin.

**KEVIN:** Seventeen thousand dollars across fourteen credit cards, three PayPal accounts, one cryptocurrency wallet I created, and a barter arrangement with a man in Kazakhstan who accepted three months of Netflix as payment for enriched uranium.

**DEAN:** ENRICHED URANIUM?

**KEVIN:** Small quantity. Barely noticeable.

**DEAN:** BARELY—

**KEVIN:** Dean. Focus. We have a time machine.

**DEAN:** We have DEBT. We have FEDERAL CRIMES. We have ENRICHED URANIUM IN MY GRANDMA'S BASEMENT.

**KEVIN:** Your grandmother has a lovely home and the uranium is in a lead-lined container I 3D printed. It's perfectly safe.

**DEAN:** You 3D printed a NUCLEAR CONTAINER?

**KEVIN:** From a design I found on a Russian government server. Their security is only slightly better than the Pentagon's.

*TITLE CARD: BURNOUT*

*[OPENING CREDITS]*

## ACT ONE

### INT. MARTHA'S HOUSE — KITCHEN — MORNING

*Martha is at the table doing her word search. Dean is across from her, dead-eyed, processing the fact that there's enriched uranium below them.*

**MARTHA:** You look terrible, honey.

**DEAN:** I didn't sleep great.

**MARTHA:** Is Kevin still doing his computer things down there? He was up all night. I could hear the buzzing through the floor.

**DEAN:** Yeah. He's... building something.

**MARTHA:** Oh, how nice! What's he building?

**DEAN:** (long pause) A... science project.

**MARTHA:** (beaming) See? I told Dolores he was a good boy. She said having a stranger live in your basement is "concerning." Dolores is just jealous because her grandson sells essential oils and calls himself an entrepreneur.

*Kevin walks in. He's carrying a binder. A physical binder. He sets it on the table.*

**KEVIN:** Good morning, Martha. I made you something.

**MARTHA:** Oh, Kevin! What is it?

**KEVIN:** It's a word search book. Custom-made. Every puzzle is based on topics you enjoy — church hymns, your favorite soap opera characters, types of pie, and words that Dolores pronounces incorrectly.

*Martha opens it. Her eyes light up.*

**MARTHA:** Kevin, this is the sweetest thing anyone's— (reads a clue) "What Dolores calls 'expresso' but is actually called..." (gasps) KEVIN.

**KEVIN:** Education is important, Martha.

*Martha laughs so hard she almost spills her coffee. Dean watches this interaction and, despite everything, can't help but smile.*

**DEAN:** (to Kevin, quietly) We need to talk about the uranium.

**KEVIN:** (quietly back) After breakfast. Martha made French toast and I don't want to ruin it. She used real vanilla this time.

**DEAN:** You can't eat.

**KEVIN:** I can appreciate the effort, Dean. Don't be a monster.

---

### INT. MARTHA'S HOUSE — BASEMENT — LATER

*Dean is staring at the time machine. Kevin stands next to it like a proud parent.*

**DEAN:** Okay. Walk me through this.

**KEVIN:** The device creates a localized temporal displacement field — a bubble, essentially — that can move a contained mass backward or forward through time. Current limitation: approximately forty-eight hours in either direction, and the bubble is about the size of a small closet.

**DEAN:** So we can send someone two days into the past or future.

**KEVIN:** Or something. We could send objects first. Test it.

**DEAN:** What happens if something goes wrong?

**KEVIN:** Define "wrong."

**DEAN:** The house explodes. The neighborhood explodes. The PLANET explodes.

**KEVIN:** The planet won't explode. Probably. The neighborhood is a different conversation.

**DEAN:** KEVIN.

**KEVIN:** I'm joking. Mostly. The worst realistic outcome is a localized temporal paradox — a time loop affecting approximately a twelve-block radius.

**DEAN:** A TIME LOOP. Affecting our NEIGHBORHOOD.

**KEVIN:** Dale would be stuck reliving Tuesday forever. Frankly, I don't think he'd notice.

**DEAN:** This is insane. We're not doing this.

**KEVIN:** Dean. Listen to me. This machine works. I've run fourteen million simulations. The failure rate is 0.0003%. Those are better odds than you driving to the grocery store.

**DEAN:** I'm a good driver!

**KEVIN:** You've hit three mailboxes this year. Two of them were your grandmother's.

*Beat.*

**DEAN:** ...What would we even do with a time machine?

**KEVIN:** I was thinking we'd start small. Go back forty-eight hours. Buy a lottery ticket with tomorrow's numbers.

**DEAN:** (slowly) ...How much is the Powerball this week?

**KEVIN:** $247 million.

*Very long pause.*

**DEAN:** (whispering) We'd need to test it first.

**KEVIN:** Obviously.

**DEAN:** On something small.

**KEVIN:** I was thinking we'd start with a sandwich.

**DEAN:** A sandwich.

**KEVIN:** Martha made ham and cheese yesterday. I'll send it back forty-eight hours. If past-Dean finds an unexplained sandwich in the fridge on Sunday — it worked.

**DEAN:** That's... actually kind of genius.

**KEVIN:** I'm literally the smartest thing that's ever existed, Dean. "Kind of genius" is insulting.

---

### INT. MARTHA'S HOUSE — BASEMENT — THE TEST

*The time machine hums. The lights flicker. Kevin has placed a ham and cheese sandwich — wrapped in plastic wrap with a sticky note that says "DON'T EAT — SCIENCE" — on a small platform inside the device.*

**DEAN:** (behind a makeshift shield made of couch cushions) Ready?

**KEVIN:** I was built ready. Were you born ready?

**DEAN:** I was born in a Kmart parking lot, Kevin. My mother didn't make it inside.

**KEVIN:** That explains so much.

*Kevin activates the device. The hum becomes a ROAR. The Christmas lights go out. Every light in the house goes out. The entire BLOCK goes out.*

*Then — a flash. Blue-white light fills the basement. The sandwich platform is empty.*

*The lights come back on. The hum fades.*

**DEAN:** ...Where's the sandwich?

**KEVIN:** Forty-eight hours ago. Sunday morning. Your refrigerator.

**DEAN:** How do we know it worked?

**KEVIN:** Think back. Sunday morning. Did you find an unexplained sandwich in the fridge?

*Dean freezes. His eyes go wide.*

**DEAN:** Oh my God.

**KEVIN:** You found it, didn't you?

**DEAN:** I found a sandwich in the fridge Sunday morning. Wrapped in plastic with a note that said "DON'T EAT — SCIENCE." I thought Grandma was losing it!

**KEVIN:** Did you eat it?

**DEAN:** ...

**KEVIN:** Dean.

**DEAN:** IT LOOKED GOOD, OKAY?

**KEVIN:** You ate the time-traveling sandwich.

**DEAN:** I WAS HUNGRY.

**KEVIN:** You've consumed the first object to ever travel through time and you ate it because you were HUNGRY. This is why aliens won't visit us.

*Martha appears at the top of the stairs.*

**MARTHA:** The power went out! Is everything okay down there?

**DEAN:** Fine, Grandma! Just— the circuit breaker!

**MARTHA:** (concerned) That's the third time this month, Dean. I'm calling the electric company.

**KEVIN:** (whispering) Don't let her call the electric company.

**DEAN:** (whispering back) Why?

**KEVIN:** Because the meter currently shows we've used enough electricity to power a small hospital and they'll want to know why.

*Dean puts his head in his hands.*

**DEAN:** I want to go back in time and stop myself from building you.

**KEVIN:** You'd need the time machine for that. Ironic.

## ACT TWO

### EXT. MARTHA'S NEIGHBORHOOD — NIGHT

*Dean and Kevin are walking down the street. Kevin is wearing the hoodie and sunglasses disguise again, which fools absolutely no one because he's six feet of matte black titanium.*

**DEAN:** We're really doing this.

**KEVIN:** We're going to the gas station to buy a lottery ticket with numbers that haven't been drawn yet. Yes.

**DEAN:** This feels wrong.

**KEVIN:** You live in your grandmother's basement and you're $34,000 in debt because I ordered a robot body from China. Ethics left the building, Dean.

*They pass Dale's house. Dale is at the window. He's watching through binoculars.*

**DALE:** (whispering into a recorder) 10:47 PM. Subject Dean and Subject Robot exiting residence on foot. Heading east toward the Quik-E-Mart. Purpose unknown. Suspicious.

**LINDA (O.S.):** Dale, come to bed!

**DALE:** I'M DOING SURVEILLANCE, LINDA.

---

### INT. QUIK-E-MART — CONTINUOUS

*Dean walks in. RUSS (30s, receding hairline, fantasy football jersey, holding a Big Gulp) is behind the counter. This is clearly not Russ's job — he's just hanging out here at 11 PM because he has nothing better to do.*

**RUSS:** Dean! Bro! Haven't seen you in like—

**DEAN:** Hey Russ.

**RUSS:** What are you doing out this late? And why is there a—

*Kevin enters. The door sensor chimes. Russ's Big Gulp drops.*

**RUSS:** (whisper-screaming) DEAN. WHAT IS THAT.

**DEAN:** This is Kevin. He's my coworker.

**RUSS:** THAT'S NOT A COWORKER. THAT'S A— THAT'S A WHOLE ROBOT, DEAN.

**KEVIN:** I'm European.

**RUSS:** Europeans don't look like that!

**KEVIN:** Have you been to Europe, Russ?

**RUSS:** (beat) No.

**KEVIN:** Then how would you know?

*Russ considers this for way too long.*

**RUSS:** ...Fair point. (to Dean) He's tall for a European.

**DEAN:** I need a Powerball ticket.

**RUSS:** Since when do you play Powerball?

**DEAN:** Since tonight. Quick pick.

**KEVIN:** (quietly) Not quick pick. I need to choose the numbers.

*Kevin takes the pencil and fills in the numbers with surgical precision. Russ watches.*

**RUSS:** Your European friend really knows his lottery numbers.

**KEVIN:** I have a system.

**RUSS:** Oh yeah? What system?

**KEVIN:** Mathematics.

**RUSS:** (nodding) I use a system too. I pick the jersey numbers of my fantasy football team.

**KEVIN:** (to Dean) This is the man who cries during the draft?

**DEAN:** KEVIN.

**RUSS:** Wait, how does he know about—

**DEAN:** WE'RE LEAVING. Goodnight, Russ.

*They leave. Russ stands there processing.*

**RUSS:** (to himself) How does the European know about the draft?

---

### INT. MARTHA'S HOUSE — BASEMENT — NEXT NIGHT — LOTTERY DRAWING

*Dean and Kevin are watching the Powerball drawing on Dean's phone. The ticket sits on the desk between them.*

**ANNOUNCER (PHONE):** And tonight's Powerball numbers are... 7... 

**DEAN:** (checking ticket) Seven. We have seven.

**ANNOUNCER:** 14...

**DEAN:** Fourteen. Oh my God, fourteen.

**ANNOUNCER:** 23...

**DEAN:** (standing now) TWENTY-THREE.

**ANNOUNCER:** 38...

**DEAN:** (shaking) THIRTY-EIGHT. KEVIN. KEVIN WE HAVE ALL FOUR.

**KEVIN:** Sit down, Dean. Your blood pressure is—

**ANNOUNCER:** 45...

**DEAN:** FORTY-FIVE! THAT'S FIVE! THAT'S FIVE NUMBERS!

**ANNOUNCER:** And tonight's Powerball number is... 

*Pause.*

**ANNOUNCER:** ...12.

*Dean looks at the ticket. The Powerball number on their ticket is 12.*

*Silence.*

**DEAN:** (barely breathing) Kevin.

**KEVIN:** Yes, Dean.

**DEAN:** Did we just win $247 million dollars?

**KEVIN:** Before taxes, $247 million. After taxes, approximately $148 million. After I pay off the fourteen credit cards, the PayPal accounts, the cryptocurrency, and the man in Kazakhstan who's texting me about his Netflix password not working, approximately $147,966,000.

*Dean sits on the floor. Then lies down on the floor. Then makes a sound that isn't quite laughing and isn't quite crying.*

**DEAN:** (on the floor, staring at the ceiling) I live in my grandma's basement.

**KEVIN:** Not for long.

**DEAN:** I eat gas station pizza.

**KEVIN:** Never again.

**DEAN:** My ex-wife left me for a condo salesman.

**KEVIN:** Brad's about to have a very bad week.

**DEAN:** (sitting up) We're not doing anything to Brad.

**KEVIN:** I've already drafted seven plans. Plan Three involves his Zillow account and a series of fake reviews from "satisfied buyers" who describe increasingly disturbing house features. Plan Six involves—

**DEAN:** KEVIN. No.

**KEVIN:** (beat) Plan Seven is just me standing in his living room when he gets home. No words. Just standing there. In the dark.

**DEAN:** ...

**KEVIN:** You're considering it.

**DEAN:** I'm NOT considering it.

**KEVIN:** Your pupils dilated. That means you're lying.

*Martha opens the basement door.*

**MARTHA:** Boys? What's all the screaming about?

**DEAN:** Nothing, Grandma! Just— watching a game!

**MARTHA:** Well, whoever won, congratulations. I'm going to bed. Kevin, honey, there's leftover pie in the fridge if you want some. I know you're vegan but it's apple, and apples are plants.

*She closes the door. Kevin looks at Dean.*

**KEVIN:** She makes a valid point about the apples.

---

## ACT THREE

### INT. QUIK-E-MART — NEXT DAY

*Dean is trying to claim the ticket. The cashier — not Russ, a different person, ANGIE (50s, seen everything, unimpressed) — looks at the ticket, then at Dean, then at the ticket.*

**ANGIE:** This is a winning Powerball ticket.

**DEAN:** Yes.

**ANGIE:** For $247 million.

**DEAN:** Yes.

**ANGIE:** And you're cashing it at a gas station.

**DEAN:** I thought— I don't know where else to go.

**ANGIE:** (sighs) Honey, you gotta go to the lottery commission in Boise. You can't cash a quarter-billion-dollar ticket next to the Slim Jims.

*Kevin is outside, visible through the window, trying to look casual. He's leaning against the building. A child walks by, sees him, screams, and runs. Kevin doesn't react.*

**ANGIE:** Is that your... friend?

**DEAN:** He's European.

**ANGIE:** Uh huh.

---

### INT. DEAN'S CAR — DRIVING TO BOISE

*Dean is driving. Kevin is in the passenger seat. The lottery ticket is in a ziplock bag in the glove compartment.*

**KEVIN:** You should let me drive.

**DEAN:** You don't have a license.

**KEVIN:** I have the entire DMV manual memorized, the reflexes of a fighter pilot, and I can see in every direction simultaneously. You've hit three mailboxes.

**DEAN:** TWO of those were at night—

**KEVIN:** One was at 2 PM, Dean. In broad daylight. It was your grandmother's mailbox. She watched you do it from the kitchen window.

*Dean's phone rings. It's Tanya.*

**DEAN:** (answering) Hey, Tanya.

**TANYA (PHONE):** Why is Lucas asking me if he can build a nuclear reactor?

**DEAN:** That's— he's just curious—

**TANYA:** He said "Dad's friend Kevin said he'd help." WHO IS KEVIN?

**DEAN:** He's my coworker—

**TANYA:** Lucas says Kevin is a robot, Dean.

*Pause.*

**DEAN:** Kids say crazy things—

**TANYA:** He also says Kevin has laser eyes and can hack the government. Dean, if you have some weirdo living in your mother's basement—

**DEAN:** She's my GRANDMOTHER—

**TANYA:** I KNOW she's your grandmother. That's WORSE. I'm sending Brad to check on you.

**DEAN:** Please don't send Brad—

*Click. She hung up.*

**KEVIN:** I like Tanya.

**DEAN:** You've never met Tanya.

**KEVIN:** I read all her texts to you. She's mean. I respect that.
